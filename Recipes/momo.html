Momo (also called dumplings/dimsum)

Momo is a Indo - Tibetian cuisine freakingly popular in most of the BIMSTEC countries.

It's creation dates back to the 13th century in Tibet where it was prepared as a celebaratory dish for crop festivals.Later on with the onset of Tibetian refugees in India, it got mixed into the Indian street food cult with its strinkingly addicitve taste and comparatively healthier way of preparation thru steaming

It is simply a stylised spherical refined flour ball with a filling of either meat or veggies or cottage cheese which is eaten eith a spicy hot red chutney for the thrill and mayonnaise for cooling off the tongue.

With its increasing popularity, starting especially in Delhi, it also got some Indian adaptations inculcated with it with garnered great popularity. Some of these include kurkure momo, fried momo(oldest one), tandoori momo, afghani momo, etc. Currently, it is highly popular pan-India especially tier 2 cities showing major boost in its sales.

Currently our focus in on making the elegant and original version - The Tibetian Momo. Once this is mastered, the other variations will just be a matter of tweaking the final touching of the recipe.Let's start on this journey!!

Ingredients:(Serves 2)

  • 125 g Refined Flour
  • 100 g Ground Meat (or Vegetables)
  • 2 tsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1 tsp Red Chutney
  • 2 tbsp Mayonnaise
  • Salt to the taste
  • 1/2 tbsp oil
  • 1 clove of minced garlic
  • 1/2 tsp Black Pepper
  • 1tbsp soy sauce
  • 1/2 chopped cabbage
  • 1/4 chopped carrot

Preparation:

  1. Mix the all-purpose flour, salt, and oil in a bowl. Gradually add water and knead for about 5 minutes until it reaches a soft, pliable, and non-sticky texture. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 20 to 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Sauté the garlic for about 30 seconds. Add your chopped vegetables and toss on high flame for 3 minutes until slightly tender but still crunchy. Stir in the soy sauce, salt, black pepper, and spring onion greens. Turn off the heat and allow the mixture to cool completely to room temperature before shaping.
  3. Divide the rested dough into small equal-sized balls (about ≈ 20 gms each). Take one ball and roll it out into a thin, 4 to 5-inch disc. Place a teaspoon of the cooled filling in the center. Fold the edges into pleats, bringing them together at the top, and pinch tightly to seal the dumpling.
  4. Place the shaped momos in an oiled steamer or an idli pot with boiling water, making sure they are spaced out. Steam for about 5 to 8 minutes until the outer wrapper becomes translucent and firm.
  5. Alternatively, you can shallow-fry the steamed momos in a little oil until they develop a golden-brown, crispy exterior and garnish them with chopped green onion leaves.
  6. Serve the momos hot with red chutney and mayonnaise on the side for dipping . Enjoy your delicious homemade momos!
