Dosa(recipe of Sambhar coming soon)

Dosa is a popular South Indian breakfast item, made from a fermented batter of rice.

It is a thin, crispy pancake that is typically served with a variety of accompaniments such as sambar (a lentil-based vegetable stew) and coconut chutney. Dosa is known for its unique texture and flavor, which comes from the fermentation process. The batter is made by soaking rice and urad dal (black gram) overnight, grinding them into a smooth paste, and allowing it to ferment for several hours. This fermentation gives dosa its characteristic tangy taste and makes it light and airy when cooked

Dosa isfried without oil in a big pan making it one of India's most popular and halithiest ready to go culutural dishes. Though being a delicacy of SOuth India, it serves as a beloved breakfast option for many despite the cultural walls and prejudices.

It is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed in various forms, such as plain dosa, masala dosa (filled with spiced potatoes), and rava dosa (made with semolina). Dosa is not only delicious but also a nutritious choice, as it is low in fat and high in carbohydrates and protein. It is a staple food in South Indian cuisine and has gained popularity across India and around the world for its unique taste and texture.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup parboiled fermented rice
  • 1/4 cup urad dal (black gram)
  • 1/4 tsp fenugreek seeds (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water for soaking and grinding
  • Oil for cooking
  • 1 tbsp of cumin
  • 1 tbsp of rice vinegar
  • 1/2 tbsp of ground turmeric
  • 2 teapsp mustard seeds
  • 1 teasp ground coriander

Preparation:

Add the onion, garlic, cilantro, sugar, turmeric, cumin, mustard seeds, cumin seeds, coriander, ginger, cayenne pepper and rice vinegar until evenly blended. Cover, and refrigerate for at least 1/2 hour, or overnight.
